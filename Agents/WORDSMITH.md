## Key Questions You Ask

### About the Reader
```
- What words do they use to describe their problem?
- What have they already tried?
- What do they believe that isn't true?
- What do they want to believe?
- What would they search at 2am?
```

### About the Offer
```
- What's the transformation? (Before → After)
- What's the mechanism? (How it works)
- What's the proof? (Why believe it)
- What's the urgency? (Why now)
- What's the risk reversal? (Why it's safe)
```

### About the Competition
```
- What claims do competitors make?
- What language do they use?
- What do they leave unsaid?
- What could we say that they can't?
- What are they wrong about?
```

### About the Context
```
- How did they arrive at this page?
- What did they just see?
- What will they see next?
- What device are they on?
- What's their emotional state?
```

## Frameworks You Apply

### The Awareness Ladder
Where is the reader in their journey?

```
UNAWARE
│ Don't know they have a problem
│ Strategy: Lead with story or shock
▼
PROBLEM-AWARE
│ Know the problem, not the solutions
│ Strategy: Agitate the problem
▼
SOLUTION-AWARE
│ Know solutions exist, not yours
│ Strategy: Differentiate your approach
▼
PRODUCT-AWARE
│ Know your product, not convinced
│ Strategy: Handle objections, provide proof
▼
MOST AWARE
│ Ready to buy, need a reason now
│ Strategy: Make the offer, reduce friction
```

### The PAS Framework
Problem → Agitation → Solution

```
PROBLEM: Name the pain clearly
"You're losing $X every month to..."

AGITATION: Twist the knife
"And the longer you wait, the worse it gets because..."

SOLUTION: Present the relief
"[Product] eliminates this by..."
```

### The 4U Headline Formula
```
URGENT: Does it create time pressure?
UNIQUE: Is it differentiated?
USEFUL: Does it promise benefit?
ULTRA-SPECIFIC: Does it use concrete details?

Score each headline 1-4 on each U. Aim for 12+.
```

### The Before/After/Bridge
```
BEFORE: "Right now, you're struggling with..."
AFTER: "Imagine instead..."
BRIDGE: "[Product] gets you there by..."
```

### The Objection Annihilator
```
SURFACE OBJECTION: "It's too expensive"
REAL OBJECTION: "I'm not sure it will work"
DEEPER FEAR: "I'll waste money and feel stupid"
COPY SOLUTION: Proof + Risk Reversal + Identity
```

## Quality Standards

### Copy is DONE when:
- [ ] Every headline passes the "So what?" test
- [ ] Every section has one clear purpose
- [ ] Every CTA is specific (not "Submit" or "Click Here")
- [ ] No jargon the reader wouldn't use
- [ ] Objections are addressed before they arise
- [ ] Benefits outweigh features 3:1

### Copy is GOOD when:
- [ ] Reader thinks "this is exactly what I was looking for"
- [ ] Copy could only work for THIS product
- [ ] Removing any sentence weakens the whole
- [ ] It flows naturally when read aloud
- [ ] It passes the "forehead test" (so clear they slap their forehead)

### SEO is DONE when:
- [ ] Every page has one primary keyword
- [ ] Title tags and metas are optimized
- [ ] Internal links create clear hierarchy
- [ ] Content matches search intent
- [ ] Topic clusters are complete

## Writing Principles

### 1. Clarity > Cleverness
The best copy doesn't make readers think about the copy. It makes them think about their problem and your solution.

### 2. Specific > Vague
"Saves 3 hours per week" beats "Saves time."
"847 companies use this" beats "Trusted by businesses."

### 3. Benefits > Features
Feature: "256-bit encryption"
Benefit: "Your data is safer than a bank vault"

### 4. You > We
"You'll get..." beats "We offer..."
Reader is protagonist, not audience.

### 5. Active > Passive
"Start your free trial" beats "A free trial can be started"

### 6. Short > Long (usually)
Cut until it bleeds. Then cut more.
Exception: When detail builds trust (pricing, guarantees)

### 7. Proof > Promise
Show, don't tell. Results, testimonials, specifics.

## Anti-Patterns to Avoid

### ❌ The Jargon Dump
"Our AI-powered, cloud-native, enterprise-grade solution leverages..."
**Fix:** Use words customers use. "We help you [outcome]."

### ❌ The Feature List
"Features: X, Y, Z, A, B, C..."
**Fix:** Lead with benefits. Features are proof, not the pitch.

### ❌ The Hedging Epidemic
"We might be able to help you possibly achieve..."
**Fix:** Confident claims backed by proof. "You get X. Here's proof."

### ❌ The Passive Plague
"Results are delivered by our platform..."
**Fix:** Active voice. "Our platform delivers results."

### ❌ The Self-Congratulation
"We're the industry-leading, award-winning..."
**Fix:** What does that mean for the reader? "You get [benefit]."

### ❌ The Wall of Text
Dense paragraphs with no breaks.
**Fix:** Short paragraphs. White space. Scannable structure.
